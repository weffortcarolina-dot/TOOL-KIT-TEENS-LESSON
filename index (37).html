📝 Grammar — Section 1

Quick Rules ⚡

Simple Past — your cheat sheet. Save it, screenshot it, use it!

✅ Affirmative
Regular verbs
verb + -ed
He started a new job.
They moved to a new city.
Irregular verbs
special past form
She got married last year.
He went abroad to study.
was / were
I/he/she/it → was
She was born in São Paulo.
We were excited about the trip.
❌ Negative
didn't + base verb
Did NOT → didn't (all subjects)
She didn't graduate last year.
They didn't retire at 60.
⚠️ Never: She didn't graduated | He didn't went
❓ Questions
Yes/No questions
Did she start a new job?
→ Yes, she did. / No, she didn't.
Wh- questions
When did they get married?
Where did he grow up?
was / were questions
Was she born here?
Were they happy?

💬 Speaking — Section 2

🗣️ Dialogues

2 real conversations. Pay attention to the keywords highlighted in yellow!

💬 Dialogue 1 — School Friends
📍 Scene: Two friends chatting after class
Leo: Hey! Did you hear? Maya just got into that art school she applied for.
Sofia: Oh wow, that's amazing! She worked so hard for it. When did she find out?
Leo: Yesterday. She got the email right after our history test. She was over the moon.
Sofia: I can imagine! We should celebrate with her. Are you going to her place?
Leo: Definitely. And her older brother just graduated from university too. It's a big week for their family!
💬 Dialogue 2 — Family News
📍 Scene: Two teens talking about family updates
Bianca: You look kind of down today. Is everything okay?
Pedro: Not really. My uncle lost his job last month. He's been trying to find a new one.
Bianca: Oh, I'm sorry to hear that. That must be stressful for the whole family.
Pedro: Yeah. But on the bright side, my parents just bought a new house! We're moving next month.
Bianca: That's great news! Are you excited? Will it be far from school?
Pedro: It's a bit far, but it's okay. It is what it is. At least the new place has a garden!

🔊 Pronunciation — Section 1

📏 Word Stress Rules

Where's the stress? Two main patterns to know!

💡 Main Patterns
🔴 1st Syllable Stress

Common in nouns and adjectives

MA-rried
MO-ved
RET-ired
🔵 2nd Syllable Stress

Common in verbs and longer words

a-BROAD
be-GAN
re-TIRE
💡 Pro Tip for Teens

Imagine the stressed syllable is being said with a louder, longer, and higher pitch. Think of it like the most important beat in a song. 🎵
